welcome to terminal sessions

~ % Hello! My name is Nicole Schwartz and I'm a visual artist and coder based in Munsee Lenape territory, also known as Bedstuy, Brooklyn

~ % I started terminal sessions as a way of digging into the many complicated, often contradictory, feelings I hold around technology. While terminal sessions is a series of personal reflection, this practice is about information gathering, both external and internal. I ultimately hope to cultivate a space of collective reflection by leaning into personal unclarity.

~ % Terminal sessions is hosted on a raspberry pi currently located in my bedroom. Some parts of this site are only accessible when in proximity to this location.

~ % The goal of this is to generate thought on the concept of a "site", and what it means to be present on the internet.

~ % Additionally, the appearance of this website is partially controlled by a number of touch sensors in my home. I have realized that our sense of touch is highly unstimulated while online, and wanted to explore a way of restoring this sense of touch to a digital presense.

~ %

~ %

~ % The computer terminal is an interface for communicating with a computer. It is an essential tool used by coders for performing many computational tasks, both organizational and functional. While the word "terminal" means end, I'd like to reframe the computer terminal as an opening, an entry point for conversations with the computer.

~ % Inspired by the computer terminal, terminal sessions was cultivated as a space for sharing reflections, experiences and imaginations around the world of computing. Like computer terminal sessions, each "session" will have a temporary existence before they are closed. Together, the sessions will be a personal, growing and shrinking archive. I hope it can be a small entry point for future conversations and connections.

~ % This space is also inspired by the concept of a digital garden, meaning its design is playful, temporal, and personal. The entries will not be listed in chronological order. Rather they will appear and dissapear independently and are meant to be shared for a window. While in some ways this space will be unruly and generative, I am approaching it with care and intention. In doing so I hope to find some earthliness in the unsettling deepness of the internet.

~ %

Formlessness

Formlessness is a series of GLSL shaders made while meditating on the fluidity of identity.
For me, reimaging the body as formless is a way of healing fractures created by the commodification of human experience. I wanted these shaders to showcase beauty that is evolving and at every moment reforming.

See the Pen Formlessness by naschwar (@naschwar) on CodePen.

augmenting reality

An exploration of augmented reality beginning with a class taken with the School For Poetic Computation. While making these, I reflected on the following questions:

  • What is lost in expanding physical space with new dimensions?
  • How can augmenting reality be used for healing?
  • How do we create augmented reality that does not lead to full decoupling from reality (i.e escapism for those with the privilige of not confronting dangerous physical conditions)?
  • How do we prevent augmented reality from recreating the systems of harm present in reality?
